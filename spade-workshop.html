SPADE 2025

Scheduling & Parallelism in AI for Distributed Edges

Co-located with: IoT 2025
Date: To be announced | Location: Vienna, Austria

1. Workshop Scope & Motivation

The SPADE workshop focuses on efficient scheduling, task distribution, and parallelism for AI workloads deployed across distributed edge environments. As computationally intensive models—particularly in AI—become pervasive in real-world IoT deployments, centralized cloud computing is increasingly insufficient due to latency, privacy, and bandwidth constraints. This workshop aims to address challenges at the intersection of AI systems, edge computing, and real-time distributed intelligence, emphasizing scheduling efficiency, resource-awareness, and scalability.

2. Topics of Interest

  • Task distribution and scheduling for computationally intensive models on edge devices
  • Parallelism-aware model design for edge AI
  • Resource-aware AI deployment strategies
  • Hybrid edge-cloud execution frameworks
  • Model partitioning and distributed inference
  • Federated and decentralized scheduling approaches
  • Real-time and latency-sensitive computing at the edge
  • Benchmarking and testbeds for edge-based AI
  • Energy-efficient task coordination in IoT systems
  • Middleware and OS-level support for distributed edge AI
  • Applications in industrial IoT, surveillance, transportation, health, and smart cities
